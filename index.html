ABOUT THE CLUB

Why join us?!

Expierenced

The club was founded in 2006, making us one of the oldest STEM clubs at Ingraham High School. We are one of the few clubs at our school to have starred in the Seattle Times and the only high school rocket club in the Seattle Public Schools District.

Focuses on STEM Topics

Ingraham Rocketry has had considerable success and growth since its inception in 2006. It has grown from a small group to a robust force. Club activities, with their different and demanding performance targets, are sponsored by industry groups and NASA to serve the underlying mission of engaging students in sciences, technology, engineering, and math (STEM) fields.

Fun Challenges and Events!

Club activities include participating in the Team America Rocketry Challenge (TARC) and NASA's Student Launch Initiative (SLI). Many of our members also participate in Fire in the Sky (FITS), the premier Northwest rocket-festival.

Expanding your knowledge!

The club provides many opportunities to learn new skills and knowledge, such as basic aerodynamics, sewing, and mechanical/electrical design. Our members also learn how to work with fiberglass, plastic, wood, Kraft phenolic paper, 3D printers, and more. Club graduates have gone on to universities to study math, aerospace engineering, chemistry, biology, physics and bio-engineering, discovering career paths that didn't seem possible when they first started high school.

Donate

Rocketry is expensive. While our rockets include some very inexpensive components, such as cardboard tubes and spray paint, they also include expensive components such as altimeters and other electronic components. While the Club receives some funding from Friends of Ingraham, we also rely on community support.

Donate Button!

To ensure that all donations are tax deductible, we fundraise through the Alliance for Education, a registered 501(c)3, tax ID # 91-1508191. No fees are charged for this service; the Rocket Club receives 100% of the funds you donate
